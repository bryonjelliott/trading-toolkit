What this strategy is

A confluence day-trading approach: each morning it scans ~70 liquid Nasdaq stocks and flags only those where at least 3 of 5 independent signals agree. Stacking confirmations filters out weak, low-probability trades — you trade only where trend, momentum, volume and the broad market all line up.

The 5 confluence signals

  • 1Price near support / resistance — within 1.5% of a key level price has reversed at twice before (swing pivots, clustered over the past year). Long near support, short near resistance; the level also anchors the stop.
  • 2EMA 9/21 alignment — EMA9 above EMA21 = uptrend (long bias); below = downtrend (short bias). A wider gap means a stronger trend.
  • 3RSI 14 filter — passes between 28 and 74. Skips overbought (≥75, chasing) and oversold (≤27, catching a falling knife).
  • 4Volume confirmation — yesterday's volume ≥ 1.1× its 20-day average. Confirms real participation behind the move.
  • 5SPY / QQQ alignment — both index trends must agree with your direction. Trade with the market, not against it.

Which direction to trade

  • Long when EMAs trend up and price sits near support.
  • Short when EMAs trend down and price sits near resistance.
  • If signals conflict, default to the SPY/QQQ market direction shown in the badge above.

The trade plan (Stage 2 builds this per stock)

Entry
Long: prior-day high + $0.05 (breakout). Short: prior-day low − $0.05 (breakdown).
Stop loss
Nearest S/R beyond entry, or 0.75× ATR — whichever is tighter. Keep stop distance 0.5–1.5× ATR.
Target 1 — 1R
Entry ± (entry − stop). Scale out 50% here.
Target 2 — 2R
Entry ± 2×(entry − stop). Exit the rest here.
Position size
Risk = 1% of account. Shares = risk ÷ stop distance (round down). Never size past 1%.

What to look for (quality & conviction)

  • Higher signal count = higher conviction — a 5/5 or 4/4 beats a borderline 3.
  • Strong gap on a catalyst (≥4%) plus high relative volume (RVOL ≥ 2×) means the stock is "in play."
  • Healthy ATR so the stop has room; lower-float names move faster and cleaner.
  • Market not fighting you — glance at the SPY/QQQ trend badge before committing.

Execution discipline

  • Wait for the entry trigger (the actual breakout/breakdown) — don't anticipate it.
  • Honor the stop. Never widen it, and never add to a loser.
  • Scale out at 1R, move the stop to breakeven, let the runner work toward 2R.
  • Risk a max of 1% per trade. A few A-setups beat many marginal ones.
